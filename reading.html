The Yoga Sutras of Pantajali

2.45 Convert every work into Yoga with the magic wand of right attitude. (p.141)

Feeling more than blessed after reading the Yoga Sutra of Patanjali. It’s a book I know I’ll return to again and again. Somehow, within its lines, you don’t just read the words, you recognize parts of your own life. You begin to live your story when you find your sentence in the book.





The Yamas & Niyamas

“When we open our eyes and see everything as an opportunity to explore and to learn, nothing becomes insignificantly in its ability to teach us and grow us.”

In The Yamas & Niyamas, the teachings go beyond definitions—they become something to observe in daily life. What I like about this book is how it brings awareness to what we often overlook: the quiet, unconscious ways we act, think, and respond. Yamas and Niyamas are not just ideas, but something we may already be practicing or neglecting without noticing. Through reflection and simple exploration exercises, it gently invites us to see how these principles show up in real moments, and how we might live them more intentionally, rather than just understand them intellectually. For example, finding courage, creating balance, and practicing self-love are all part of Ahimsa. We may not always be aware that when we are harsh or violent toward ourselves, it can also manifest in how we treat others. This book gently brings attention to these subtle patterns, helping us recognise that Ahimsa is not only about non-violence outwardly, but also inwardly—with how we speak to, judge, and care for ourselves. If this resonates, you might find something meaningful within its pages.
