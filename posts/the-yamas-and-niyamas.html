The Yamas & Niyamas

The practice beyond the mat, exploring life through the Yamas (ethical restraints) and Niyamas (self-observances).

The journey begins by looking inward. There, we discover the freedom to choose our attitude, our thoughts, and our actions—becoming skillful participants in the adventure of being alive.

What are Yamas and Niyamas?

The Yamas and Niyamas are the first two limbs of Patanjali's Eight Limbs of Yoga. They form the ethical foundation of the yogic path, offering timeless principles imeless principles that guide us toward living with purpose, compassion, self-awareness, and inner peace.

Yamas = relationship with the external world; Niyamas = relationship with the inner world.

Rather than a set of rules to follow, the Yamas and Niyamas are invitations to explore how we relate to ourselves, to others, and to the world around us. Each Sanskrit term carries layers of wisdom that unfold through study, self-reflection, and lived experience.

The Five Yamas: How We Relate to the World Around Us

The Yamas are often described as ethical restraints or universal values. They guide how we interact with others and how we move through the world with awareness and compassion.

  • Ahimsa — Non-violence
  • Satya — Truthfulness
  • Asteya — Non-stealing
  • Brahmacharya — Non-excess
  • Aparigraha — Non-possessiveness

The Five Niyamas: How We Relate to Ourselves

The Niyamas are often described as personal observances or inner disciplines. They guide how we relate to ourselves, encouraging self-awareness, growth, and a deeper connection with our inner world.

  • Saucha – Purity
  • Santosha – Contentment
  • Tapas – Discipline
  • Svadhyaya – Self-study
  • Ishvara Pranidhana – Surrender
